Definitions
Opening Value is the dollar value rolling into the RRIF plan.
Expected Annual Rate of Return is the expected rate to be used in the calculation.
Payment Type is the Payment Type.
Payment Per Period is the dollar amount you would like to receive as a payment. If the annual payment amount you specify is lower than the required annual minimum payment in any given year, the RRIF Calculator will increase the payment to the required minimum amount.
Payment Frequency is the frequency you would like to receive your income. Choices are Annual, Semi-Annual, Quarterly, Monthly, Bi-weekly or Bi-monthly.
Your or your spouse's Year of Birth is the date of birth of you or your spouse or common-law partner. To decrease the required minimum payment, the date of your younger spouse or common-law partner may be used.
Québec Resident is a selection option to choose if living in Québec, given withholding taxes are calculated differently than in the rest of Canada.
New or pre-existing account is the selection required whether the RRIF is new or a pre-existing account.

Help
Assumptions:
  • TFSA Plan Type can be used to illustrate withdrawals from a cash account.
  • No additional contributions are made into a TFSA or cash account.
  • Withdrawals from cash accounts do not take into account any capital gains, capital losses and individual taxes payable.
  • Assumes withdrawals start January 1st of the following year.
  • RRIF minimum withdrawal rates are based on the regulations of the federal Income Tax Act.
  • RRIF start date for new accounts is considered today with payments to start in the following year, for pre-existing accounts it is assumed to be January 1st of the current year.
  • RRIF factor is based on age as of December 31st of the previous year.
  • LIF minimum and maximum withdrawal rates are based on the regulations of the federal or provincial jurisdiction you select.
  • LIF start date for new accounts is considered today, for pre-existing accounts it is assumed to be January 1st of the current year.
  • LIF factor is based on age as of December 31st of the previous year.
  • If residing in PEI, NT, NU or YK, please select the LIF maximum withdrawal rates governed by Canadian federal legislation.

Definitions
Years Active
The number of years between the first payment year and the earlier of value depletion or annuitant age 100.
Opening Value
Value of the RRIF at inception.
Net Payout
Total amount of net payouts made to annuitant.
Taxes Withheld
Total amount of taxes withheld from payouts made to annuitant.
Balance Remaining
Displays any balance remaining when the annuitant reaches 100 years old or when the annual payment requested is higher than the RRIF value at the beginning of the year.
Investment Income is the projected total amount of investment income earned over the duration of the RRIF.
